about me

As you may have figured out, I'm Sophie. I'm currently a second-year student at McGill University, studying Computer Science and Biology. I know you're probably somewhat busy... so here's 3 quick facts about me:

  1. I recently made a switch from the health field to the tech world, and I'm loving it!
    Until a few months ago, I was never much interested in computer science, or anything tech-related for that matter. Actually, the majority of my extra-curricular activities and internships were related to public health and medicine. It was only during my internship in the cardiology department at the CRCHUM that I stumbled upon the use of technology in health sciences; more precisely, the use of artifical intelligence for the diagnosis of cardiac amyloidosis. I then began reading on machine learning and over the summer, I decided to take an introductory class to computer science. I ended up really liking it, and so here I am now!
  2. I'm a big mental health advocate.
    During my years in CEGEP, I was involved my college's Jack.org chapter. Throughout the semesters, we organized activities promoting youth mental health, such as a cultural mental health panel, informative self-care booths and summits. I was also a member of the collegeâ€™s internal Mental Health Taskforce where I led a focus groups alongside Student Services staff to survey student needs.
  3. I speak 4 languages.
    Growing up in Montreal, I'm fluent in both French and English. I know enough Spanish and Vietnamese. Bonus points! I dabble in ASL, and I took 4 years of Latin in high school.

Leucan

Leucan is a charity supporting cancer-stricken children and their family. Annually, they hold a Shave-to-Save Challenge where people can choose to cut their hair and collect donations; the money raised help fund different projects, such as playrooms in hospital, school awareness programs and advancing clinical research.

Jack.org

Jack.org is a Canadian organization empowering youth to take action on mental health. They do this mainly through their talks, chapters and summits. When I was involved in my college's chapter, we organized self-care booths, discussion panels about mental illness & more. Jack.org does a really great work when it comes to supporting members of their network through funding and training.

Le Chainon

Le Chainon provides accomodation and tailored support for women in difficult situations. The charity assists women experiencing "personal, social and economic hardship." Their main source of funding is their store, Le Magasin le Chainon where volunteers are welcome to help out with customer service and the sorting of supplies.

Global Brigades

Global Brigades is an international non-profit working towards "resolving health and economic disparities." Unlike some organizations promoting voluntourism, GB has implemented a hollistic model that works towards building community ownership and sustainability. When I was involved with my chapter, we raised more than $50,000 for the cause and collected large amount of health supplies, such as toothbrushes and soap. We volunteered in rural communities in Honduras alongside local leaders on community development projects.
